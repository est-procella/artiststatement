The second exploration of transformation concentrates of the ‘spaciality’… these explorations usually connect to parts of the space occupied by the
suspension of objects, projections, placements according to natural light. These explorations question the function of art if it were just an object.
While design is entirely non-essential to life, it brings a sense of arbitrary elation that we experience constantly to the point that we cannot live
without it. These explorations aim to encourage the viewer to celebrate the possibilities of a non-art space in an art context, to celebrate design in
daily life. Digital media aids such transformations in presenting portals to unfamiliar experiences for a viewer to connect to. To build a connection,
familiarity and unfamiliarity must be balanced. Language is used to convey generally familiar narratives of experiences for unfamiliar spaces to
encourage the viewer to form a connection.
